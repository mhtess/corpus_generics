Stanford CoCoLab

You will read short dialogues and answer questions about them. There are 2 parts to this experiment, each with trials.
The HIT should take about 10 minutes.

Instructions: Part 1

This experiment has 2 parts to it. In this part, you will read dialogues between two people. One of the words or phrases will be underlined, as in the following short snippet:

Speaker #1: "I was reading the other day about how birds reproduce."
Speaker #2: "I know that birds lay eggs. What else is there to it?"

You will be asked to generate words or phrases that refer to the same kind of thing as the underlined word or phrase. You be given the appropriate category of things that your responses should be from. For example, the prompt after the dialogue may read:

are a kind of . Please list {{}} other examples of .

Here, a reasonable response could be: "Dogs", "Cats", "Fish", etc...
You may find it useful to read the whole dialogue to gain a better understanding the underlined word or phrase.

Instructions: Part 2

In this 2nd and last part, you will see the same dialogues as during Part 1. However, a different set of words or phrases will be underlined, as in the following short snippet:

Speaker #1: "I was reading the other day about how birds reproduce."
Speaker #2: "I know that birds lay eggs. What else is there to it?"

You will be asked to evaluate your responses from Part 1 in terms of how likely each is to have the property, using a slider bar. For example, the prompt after the dialogue may read:

For each of the following types of animals, how likely is it that they lay eggs?

very unlikelyvery likely
We have put the slider for "Fish" around the middle of the bar because "laying eggs" is only something female fish can do. So, if you were to come across a random fish, it's equally likely that they do or do not lay eggs (because it just depends if it's a male or female). Please try to analyze your intuitions as best you can to give a reliable response.
