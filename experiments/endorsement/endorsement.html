Instructions

In this experiment, you will read dialogues between two people. One phrase or sentence will be underlined, as in the following short snippet:

Speaker #1: "I was reading the other day about how birds reproduce."
Speaker #2: "I know that birds lay eggs. What else is there to it?"

You will be asked to interpret this statement by rating how many individual members of the category, the speaker believes, have the property (for example: how many individual birds "lay eggs"), using a slider bar. For example, the prompt after the dialogue may read:

What percentage of individual birds does the speaker think "lay eggs"?

0%100%
For this example, we have put the slider around the middle of the bar because "laying eggs" is only something female birds can do. So, if you were to come across a random, individual bird, it's equally likely that they do or do not lay eggs (because it depends if it's a male or female). Please try to analyze your intuitions as best you can to give a reliable response.
